## 🔍📄 Background ##
Based in Chicago, Cyclistic is a bike-share company which launched a successful bike-share offering in 2016 with the fleet of bikes rising to 5824. The bikes are geotracked and are easily accessed across 692 stations. A rider can get a bike from one station and return it to any other station in the system at any time. The convenience offered by Cyclistic attracts numerous riders who in turn contribute to the overall growth of the company.

So far, Cyclistic marketing strategy has heavily focused on building general awareness and appealing to broad customer segments. The one effective tactic that has been deployed is the flexible pricing plans catering for casual riders (those with single-ride passes or full-day passes) and member riders (those with annual memberships). The company has also taken into consideration inidividuals with disabilities and those who can't operate two-wheeled bicycles by offering reclining bikes, cargo bikes and hand tricycles. The company's data indicates that majority of the riders prefer traditional bikes while only 8% make use of the assistive options. In addition, while there is a likelihood of Cyclistic users to ride for leisure, about 30% use the bikes to commute to work each day. 

The marketing director strongly believes that maximizing annual memberships is the key to future company growth and success. The task now falls to my team and I (a junior data analyst) to try and understand the behavioral differences in bike use between casual riders and annual members. From the insights, we will be able to craft a suitable marketing strategy to convert casual riders into annual members.
